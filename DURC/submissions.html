The Dragon: Drexel’s Undergraduate Research Journal is proud to announce the inaugural call for papers for the fall 2015 volume.

This new journal’s mission is to showcase the research of Drexel undergraduates across all disciplines. As the only peerreviewed journal of undergraduate research at Drexel, the Dragon introduces students to the scholarly publication and review process. The Dragon also serves as a venue for the sharing of knowledge at Drexel, allowing students an opportunity to communicate with other undergraduates with an interest in research so that they may develop the skills related to the production of knowledge and gain a greater understanding of the world outside of their fields of study. The Dragon is an online, annual publication of research performed by Drexel undergraduate students. The Dragon is a publication of the Drexel Undergraduate Research Council, a student organization whose goal is to increase access to research opportunities at Drexel.

Submission requirements

Submissions should be between 1500 and 2000 words and be accompanied by a 150 to 200 word abstract, reporting the work of a Drexel undergraduate. Complete submission guidelines can be found at http://goo.gl/vXEd6q Manuscripts should be emailed to submissions.thedragon@gmail.com and named using the following convention: PrimaryAuthorSurname_Running_Head. doc (or .docx).

Submission Deadline: October 17, 2015 by 11:59pm

All papers will undergo a rapid peerreview process by Drexel undergraduates, graduate students, and faculty, with feedback provided to authors within three weeks of submission. However, failure to follow submission guidelines may result in rejection without review.
